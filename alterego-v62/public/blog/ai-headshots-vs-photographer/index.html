AI Headshots vs Traditional
Photographer — Is It Worth It?

May 2026 · 5 min read

A professional headshot session in Australia typically costs between $300 and $800. You book weeks in advance, spend an hour in a studio, get 3-5 edited images back a week later, and hope one of them captures you the way you want to be seen.

AI headshots cost less than $1 per image. You upload a selfie, pick a style, and have a result in 30 seconds. But are they actually good enough to replace the real thing?

The honest answer is: it depends entirely on what you need them for.

The case for AI headshots

Cost. There's no comparison. A professional session costs 300-800x more than a single AI generation. For someone updating their LinkedIn photo, testing a new look for their personal brand, or needing a quick professional image for a bio or speaking engagement, the economics of AI are impossible to argue with.

Speed. 30 seconds versus 2-3 weeks including booking, shooting and editing turnaround. If you need something today, AI is your only option.

Iteration. With a photographer you get one session, one lighting setup, one day. With AI you can generate 20 variations, try different styles, and pick the one that feels right. That creative freedom has real value.

No camera anxiety. A lot of people freeze in front of a camera. The AI works from a casual selfie taken at home — no studio lights, no photographer watching you, no pressure to perform.

The case for a traditional photographer

Absolute realism. For roles where the headshot will be scrutinised up close — acting, modelling, senior executive profiles — a photograph taken by a skilled photographer with professional lighting and retouching is still the gold standard. AI is excellent but it is AI, and some audiences will notice.

Licensing and credibility. Some publications, agencies and corporate directories specifically require actual photographs. For those contexts, AI-generated images may not be acceptable.

Full creative control. A photographer can execute a very specific brief — a particular background, a particular expression, a particular outfit. AI has more variability and you can't fully control every element of the output.

Our verdict

For the vast majority of use cases — LinkedIn profiles, personal websites, social media bios, speaker profiles, email signatures — AI headshots are not just good enough, they're arguably better. The ability to iterate quickly, try different looks and generate a professional result in 30 seconds without booking a session is a genuine advantage.

For high-stakes professional contexts where the image will be scrutinised at large scale or where photographic authenticity is a requirement, a traditional photographer remains the right choice.

The smart approach is to use both for what they're each best at. Use AI for volume, speed and experimentation. Use a photographer for your definitive portrait — the one that goes on your company website or your acting portfolio.
